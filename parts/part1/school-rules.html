Q1 Are there any rules for students at your school?

Option 1: High School Context

"Yes, there are quite a few. My school is very strict about discipline. For example, we have a mandatory dress code, so we have to wear a uniform every day. Also, mobile phones are strictly prohibited; if you get caught, it gets confiscated."

Option 2: University Context

"Yes, but they are quite reasonable. The main rules revolve around academic integrity. Apart from that, we are expected to be punctual for lectures, but we don't have strict rules about what to wear."

Mandatory: Compulsory / Must do
Prohibited: Banned / Not allowed
Confiscated: Taken away by authority
Academic integrity: Being honest / No cheating
Punctual: Being on time

Q2 Do you think students would benefit more from more rules?

Advocate for Freedom

"I don't think so. I believe that if there are too many rules, students can feel suffocated, which might make them more rebellious. Schools should focus on self-discipline rather than forcing obedience."

Advocate for Order

"In some cases, yes. Young students need clear boundaries to help them focus. Without strict rules regarding behavior, a classroom can become chaotic, making it impossible to learn."

Suffocated: Feeling trapped / controlled
Rebellious: Fighting against authority
Self-discipline: Controlling yourself
Boundaries: Limits / Lines not to cross
Chaotic: Messy / No order

Q3 Do you have a teacher who does his or her job very well?

The Inspiring Teacher

"My history teacher is fantastic. He is incredibly passionate about his subject, which makes his lessons really engaging. He is also very approachable if you have questions."

The Caring Teacher

"My English professor is wonderful because she is extremely patient. She is strict but fair, and she genuinely cares about our progress."

Passionate: Loving what you do
Engaging: Holding your attention
Approachable: Easy to talk to
Strict but fair: Rules followed, but treats all equally
