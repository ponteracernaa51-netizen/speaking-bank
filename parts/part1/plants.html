Focus: Clear communication, correct grammar, good topic vocabulary, and giving reasons for your answers.

1. Do you keep plants?

"Yes, I do. I have a few potted plants in my living room and on my balcony. I think they make the atmosphere in the house much more relaxing and welcoming, although I sometimes forget to water them on time!"

2. Have you ever had a plant?

"Yes, I have. When I moved into my first apartment, I bought a cactus because I heard they were very low maintenance. It was the only plant I could keep alive because I didn't have much free time to take care of it."

3. Do you know anything about growing a plant?

"I know the basics. For example, I understand that plants need sunlight, good soil, and the right amount of water. If I buy a new plant, I usually search on Google to find out exactly how to look after it properly."

4. What plants did you grow when you were younger?

"When I was a child, my parents had a small garden. We used to grow vegetables like tomatoes and peppers. I remember helping my mother water them in the summer. It was fun to eat food that we had grown ourselves."

5. Do people in your country send plants as gifts?

"Yes, it is quite common, especially for housewarming parties. People often prefer giving a potted plant instead of a bouquet of flowers because a plant lasts much longer. It is seen as a nice decoration for a new home."

Key B2 Vocabulary

  • Atmosphere: The mood of a place.
  • Low maintenance: Easy to care for.
  • The basics: Essential knowledge.
  • Housewarming party: A party when moving into a new house.
