Examiner Tip: Subtle Differences

If the examiner asks both questions below, use different vocabulary to show your range:
"What do you do..." = Routine / Habits.
"What do you like to do..." = Preferences / Happiness.

1 What do you usually do / like to do during a break?

Physical Movement

"I usually try to get away from my desk. Since I spend a lot of time sitting down, I like to stretch my legs or go for a short stroll outside to get some fresh air. It helps clear my head."

Digital Relaxation

"I mostly just check my phone. I like to catch up on social media or reply to messages. It helps me switch off from work mode for a few minutes and just zone out."

Socializing

"I prefer to chat with my colleagues. We usually grab a coffee and gossip. Itâ€™s a nice way to unwind and have a laugh before getting back to work."

Stroll: Relaxed walk Switch off: Stop thinking about stress Zone out: Relax completely Unwind: Relax after hard work

2 How often do you take a break?

Regularly (Structured)

"I try to take one every hour. I find that if I work for too long without stopping, I lose focus. So, I take a five-minute break to recharge my batteries."

Rarely (Workaholic)

"Not often enough. When I am really engrossed in my work, I tend to lose track of time. I know itâ€™s bad, but I just want to get things done."

Recharge my batteries: Get energy back
Engrossed: Totally involved
Lose track of time: Not realize time passing
